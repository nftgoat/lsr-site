About Me


Laura Sobbott Ross is the author of five poetry collections: To the Patron Saint of Wayward Daughters (Kelsay Press 2021), The Trees Will Remain (Bell Ring Books 2019), The Graffiti of Pompeii (Adelaide Books 2018), My Mississippi (Anchor & amp; Plume Press 2016), and A Tiny Hunger (YellowJacket Press 2011). Her poems have been featured on Verse Daily and have appeared in Meridian, 32 Poems, Florida Review, Blackbird, Main Street Rag, National Poetry Review, and elsewhere.

She was named the inaugural poet laureate of Lake County in Florida. Literary prizes and awards are as follows: 2022 Bedford Competition, Finalist; 2021 International Poetry Contest for the Atlanta Review, Merit Award; 2019 Richard Snyder Publication Prize, Finalist; 2019 Washington Prize at the Word Works; Semi-finalist; 2017 Southern Humanities Review Auburn Witness Poetry Prize, First Place; 2017 Public Poetry Contest, Finalist; 2016 Anzaldua Poetry Prize, Finalist; 2016 Arts&Letters Poetry Prize, Finalist; 2015 Cape Cod Cultural Center Poetry Prize, Finalist; 2015 Tusculum Review Poetry Chapbook, Finalist; The Ledge Poetry Award 2013, First Place; Yellow Jacket Press Chapbook Contest for Florida Poets 2011, First Place; Thomas Merton Poetry of the Sacred Contest 2011, Honorable Mention; Creekwalker Poetry Prize 2008, Finalist.

She has worked as a teacher and a writing coach in Florida.
